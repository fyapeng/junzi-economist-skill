# Round 17 — dynamic verification pressure test and strategic backtrack

## Initial purpose

The round attempted to close the remaining dynamic structural-estimation audit gap after the substantive NFXP, CCP, transition, local-rank, restricted-domain, and policy-accounting objects already reproduced. Blind endpoints successively exposed incomplete policy-field binding, ambiguity between a frozen contract and a runtime certificate, cyclic final-file hashing, and a failure-injection stub that returned nonzero without exercising the real predicate.

The reusable changes were committed in `94a5d4d`, `4ca8272`, `fd1d428`, `b7f9b49`, and `47101d9`. They distinguish finite saved policies from arbitrary-input claims, add a release-grade structural verification gate, require field-to-predicate closure, separate contract–manifest–verification–report–receipt stages, and require a failing fixture to traverse the normal predicate path.

## What the endpoints established

Across the health-visit and training-entry endpoints, fresh audits repeatedly reproduced the substantive economic and numerical objects: controlled transitions, distinct NFXP and CCP estimates, all production starts and KKT decisions, Bellman residuals, local-rank language, every finite restricted-domain row, saved policy accounts, generated policy-interface calls, welfare identities, and headline values.

The endpoints remained mixed for progressively narrower release-process reasons. Those failures are useful design evidence, but they did not change the economic result, claim boundary, or next research action.

## Principal-contradiction correction

Continuing to generate new endpoints after the core result stabilized shifted resources from the main objective—building a broadly capable economist—to recursive verification of metadata and the verifier itself. The user identified this drift directly. The active endpoint was stopped, and the temporary packages were not promoted into permanent strict-execution records.

This is a real failure of stage judgment, not a request for another validation layer. It generated three general changes:

1. identify the current principal contradiction before allocating research effort;
2. use exploration, research-evidence, and public-release verification tiers;
3. after two iterations that change only secondary diagnostics or meta-process, preserve the gap and backtrack unless safety or an explicit release requirement makes it decisive.

## Decision

Stop the dynamic release-verifier branch. Keep the release gate as an opt-in instrument for public or high-consequence artifacts, not the default structural workflow. Keep `structural_estimation` partial in the formal completion audit: substantive coverage is strong, but the round does not convert a series of mixed release packages into a clean behavior claim. Resume the mainline through economic-theory breadth, real research workflows, data and computation, and gradual legacy-skill replacement.
