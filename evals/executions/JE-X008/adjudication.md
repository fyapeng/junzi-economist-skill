# Adjudication

The independent audit's first defect is not sustained by a fresh retrieval of the cited artifact. At the time of adjudication, a direct download from

`https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w30380/w30380.pdf`

returned a PDF whose cover states `August 2022, Revised April 2026`. Its SHA-256 was:

`f1a5345821cda6bf9ddc7d8b7e8d27341eb30f102d16b1da00c34dcd667ccc85`

The NBER landing page and some cached renderings still exposed May 2024 metadata. The response was therefore justified in reporting the PDF/header conflict, although it should have recorded the downloaded file hash and retrieval timestamp.

The remaining audit findings stand. The response conflated the Chinese notice's document date with webpage publication date, treated an author-page date as a manuscript revision date, overstated coverage in its stopping rationale, and sometimes used abstract verification more strongly than warranted. Overall verdict remains `mixed`.
