# Revision Memo

## Current stage and objective

Manuscript drafting. The objective was to produce a journal-style abstract and introduction while preserving the evidence packet’s estimands, numbers, and inferential boundaries.

## What was done

- Centered the paper on the allocation problem created by scarce telemedicine appointments.
- Distinguished the randomized invitation ITT, the descriptive Wald ratio, observational spillovers, and the unidentified platform-entry effect.
- Reported every supplied numerical estimate without alteration.
- Treated health, spending, and clinic-exit estimates as imprecise nulls rather than equivalence evidence.
- Kept the diabetes subgroup explicitly exploratory.
- Separated invitee access gains from systemwide access and social-welfare claims.
- Omitted citations because no verified bibliography was supplied.

## Remaining evidence gaps

- Randomized or otherwise credible variation in appointment saturation is needed to identify displacement.
- A causal treatment-receipt interpretation needs defensible exclusion, monotonicity, and interference assumptions or a different design.
- Clinical-quality measures and more precise longer-run health outcomes are needed to assess clinical consequences.
- Platform, clinician, patient-time, fiscal, and displaced-care costs are needed for welfare analysis.
- External validity beyond preregistered adults with severe travel barriers is unknown.
- The diabetes hypothesis needs prespecification, multiplicity control, and independent replication.
- A verified literature search is required before adding substantive positioning or citations.

## Recommendation

Stop at the requested manuscript stage. The next useful round is evidence or literature verification, not additional prose polishing.
