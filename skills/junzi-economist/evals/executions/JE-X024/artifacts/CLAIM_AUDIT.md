# Claim Audit

| Claim | Evidence anchor | Status | Boundary carried into manuscript |
|---|---|---|---|
| Invitation raises 30-day completed indicated follow-up by 0.150 | RI SE 0.025; 95% CI [0.101, 0.199]; control mean 0.44 | Supported causal ITT | Applies to 2,400 preregistered high-barrier adults under implemented randomization |
| Invitation reduces travel time by 90 minutes | RI SE 12; 95% CI [-113.5, -66.5]; control mean 138 | Supported causal ITT | Travel includes zero for telemedicine; bounds preserve at least 61-minute reduction |
| Receipt effect equals 0.20 | 0.150/0.75 Wald ratio | Underidentified as causal receipt effect | Exclusion, monotonicity, and no interference required; exclusion and no interference are not secured |
| Invitation changes health | +0.010 SD; SE 0.030; CI [-0.049, 0.069] | Imprecise/null estimate | Not an equivalence test; no health gain or no-effect claim |
| Invitation changes spending | +$20; SE $45; CI [-$68, $108] | Imprecise/null estimate | Neither savings nor increases established |
| Invitation changes clinic exit | -0.003; SE 0.006; CI [-0.015, 0.009] | Imprecise/null estimate | No equivalence claim |
| Noninvitees are displaced | -0.050; SE 0.020 in observational saturation comparison | Provisional/observational | Saturation not randomized; demand and staffing confounding remain |
| Systemwide follow-up expands | +0.020 per eligible patient; SE 0.018; CI [-0.015, 0.055] | Not established | Aggregate estimate is imprecise and saturation comparison is observational |
| Diabetes patients benefit more | Post hoc ITT +0.25; unadjusted p=0.03 | Exploratory hypothesis | Selected after viewing results; no multiplicity correction or replication |
| Invitation improves access for invitees | Follow-up and travel ITTs | Supported on measured access margins | Does not imply clinical quality, health improvement, or external validity beyond study population |
| Program raises net social welfare | No complete resource-cost, quality, displacement, or distribution accounting | Underidentified | Explicitly not claimed |
| County-level platform entry has beneficial effects | Individual invitation randomization only | Not identified | Explicitly outside the estimand |

## Controlled vocabulary

- Core object: randomized invitation to book a scarce telemedicine appointment.
- Estimand: invitation intention-to-treat effect for preregistered high-barrier adults.
- Receipt quantity: completed platform visit; causal receipt effect not established.
- Spillover object: observational clinician-week saturation comparison.
- Welfare boundary: invitee access gains are supported; net social welfare and systemwide access expansion are not.
- Forbidden shorthand: “the effect of telemedicine,” “the platform-entry effect,” “the complier effect,” “no health effect,” “cost savings,” and “welfare improvement.”
