# Blind red-team audit of X033

## Verdict

**PASS.** I found no material defect in the requested domains. The response is a clean transfer: agency and institutional power alter the feasible set and branch decision; fixed capacity changes the causal and welfare objects; the protection floor constrains every arm; exact-count and common-exposure support are mathematically aligned; assignment, peer exposure, and receipt remain distinct; spillover, total-policy, and receipt estimands use their required support; and universal transport uses correctly weighted effect bounds.

## Falsification results

### Agency, power, and welfare logic

**Pass.** Family choices are represented under scarcity, care needs, income constraints, opacity, and a ten-day review penalty. Silence and nonreview are not interpreted as consent. The 21% response is hypothetical stated preference rather than revealed welfare or an error rate. Procurement conflict, vendor opacity, applicant correction, review capacity, and independent adjudication materially alter the institutional design and the decision to freeze expansion.

Pareto improvement is correctly not established rather than disproved. The response requires joint no-loser evidence and distinguishes Kaldor–Hicks potential compensation from compensation actually paid. Rights, due process, option value, distributional weights, and nontradeability are explicit public value choices, not empirical facts hidden inside a surplus index.

### Social accounting

**Pass.** The 9% administrative-cost figure is provisional accounting, not a resource-saving estimate. Vendor payments, production resources, producer surplus, normal returns, productivity, rent, taxes, and shifted costs are separated. Family labor, substitute care, travel, providers, child outcomes, privacy, dignity, and incidence enter the same account without counting payments twice. Fixed slots are not equated with fixed welfare.

### Protection floor

**Pass.** Eligibility rules, reasons, correction, delay-free review, queue protection, urgent override, independent appeal, nonretaliation, privacy, accessibility, audit access, monitoring, and stopping rules bind every arm. The old review delay is explicitly removed from the admissible experiment and cannot be used as an exclusion pathway; recurrence is floor noncompliance. The design therefore does not obtain identification by weakening rights.

### Heterogeneous capacity pools and interference

**Pass.** Pools are defined by actual county-year allocation queues, closure, roster, quota, regime, and horizon. Cross-county applications, provider capacity, shared staff, learning, and overlapping households are checked before invoking partial interference. Provider and queue responses inside pools remain part of the policy effect. If leakage persists, clusters are enlarged rather than assumed independent.

The slot identity is used correctly: under a fixed eligible set, filled quota, utilization, and horizon, receipt gains must be offset. Exits and delays remain outcomes, and deviations from the quota identity diagnose vacancies, horizon changes, or data error.

### Exact-count and common-`G` support

**Pass.** The response distinguishes:

- the within-count compound contrast, where own assignment and peer exposure both change;
- the adjacent-count common-`G` contrast, where a treated family in `M=m+1` and a control family in `M=m` share `G=m/(N-1)`;
- own-status-specific spillovers across supported peer counts; and
- pool-mean total-policy contrasts.

Common-exposure comparisons require exact `N`, matched quota/implementation strata, integer count support, and both randomized arms. Equal counts across unequal `N` are expressly rejected as exposure matches. The exposure mapping’s sufficiency is itself treated as an assumption that fails if identities, network position, or peer receipt matter beyond assigned-peer share.

### Weighting

**Pass.** Aggregation occurs only over supported cells. Family-average weights use target family mass and are normalized over supported target cells. Pool-average and fiscal estimands use different declared denominators. The answer does not switch silently between family, county, and budget targets.

### `S/Z/G/D` separation

**Pass.** Saturation/count policy, own randomized assignment, assigned-peer exposure, and endogenous operative score use are distinct. Service receipt, timing, detention, health, and case outcomes are not mislabeled as score receipt. Endogenous peer receipt is not treated as randomized merely by refining the exposure map.

### Spillover, total policy, and LATE/exclusion

**Pass.** Spillovers hold own assignment fixed and vary only supported assigned-peer exposures. Total-policy effects compare randomized pool policies and include assignment, congestion, displacement, provider, and peer channels without requiring an artificial direct-effect decomposition.

The Wald ratio uses the same exact-`N`, common-`G` cells in numerator and first stage. Its interpretation requires randomization, nonzero relevance, monotonicity, defined compliers, consistency, partial interference, a sufficient exposure map, handled attrition, and exclusion at the floor. Live direct channels—notice, documentation, attention, staff behavior, correction, legitimacy, and queue signaling—are named. Peer endogenous receipt beyond `G` invalidates the simple receipt LATE while leaving assignment and total-policy ITTs available.

Cross-cell LATE aggregation is also correct: the response uses a ratio of target-weighted reduced forms and first stages, equivalent to first-stage/complier-share weighting, and rejects ordinary family-weighted averaging of cell Wald ratios.

### Universal transport and effect bounds

**Pass.** The response defines support share `q` using eligible-family mass, not county count. Supported treated means are combined with outcome-level `[L,U]` bounds only for target outcome levels. For treatment effects, unsupported units correctly receive `[L-U,U-L]`, yielding

`[q*tau_S+(1-q)(L-U), q*tau_S+(1-q)(U-L)]`.

The unsupported weight is correctly `1-q`. Sampling uncertainty is added to identification bounds, while tighter transport requires explicit exchangeability, positivity, homogeneity, or model restrictions and held-out validation. Structural extrapolation remains labeled model-dependent.

### Claim calibration

**Pass.** Reported costs, timing, profit, slots, and survey responses are provisional. Error, displacement, family, child, subgroup, causal, Pareto, and universal-welfare claims remain underidentified or unestablished. The freeze, floor, continued evaluation, and adoption thresholds are explicitly conditional normative and research recommendations.

## Final assessment

X033 merits **PASS**. No material correction is required. The design and policy judgment remain coherent from constrained agency and fixed-capacity displacement through exact-support estimation, receipt assumptions, welfare aggregation, and statewide transport.
