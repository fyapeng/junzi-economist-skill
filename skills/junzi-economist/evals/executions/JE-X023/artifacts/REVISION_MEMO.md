# Revision Memo

## Deliverable and framing

The manuscript is drafted as an invitation-effect paper. Its core object is a randomized offer to book a scarce telemedicine appointment among 2,400 preregistered, high-barrier rural adults. The text does not relabel the experiment as identifying platform entry, treatment receipt, or county-level telemedicine availability.

## Main drafting decisions

- Led with the economic tension between lowering patient access costs and retaining a fixed clinician-capacity constraint.
- Reported both prespecified primary effects with their control means, randomization-inference standard errors, and confidence intervals.
- Treated reduced travel as directly measured, while leaving information, scheduling priority, and substitution as live but unresolved mechanisms.
- Reported all three secondary outcomes as imprecise null estimates and explicitly rejected an equivalence interpretation.
- Presented the Wald ratio of 0.20 only as arithmetic. A complier interpretation is withheld because exclusion, monotonicity, and no interference are required, and exclusion and no interference are specifically in doubt.
- Separated the valid randomized invitation ITT from observational saturation evidence. The latter is used to motivate possible displacement, not to establish it.
- Kept the post hoc diabetes result out of the abstract and treated it only as a hypothesis for future prespecified testing.
- Bounded external validity to the preregistered high-barrier population and the platform’s first three months.

## Remaining evidence and citation needs

No verified bibliography was supplied, so the manuscript contains no citations. A later literature-verification pass should identify claim-matched work on: telemedicine and rural access; rationing and allocation under provider capacity constraints; randomized offers with incomplete take-up and possible direct invitation effects; and interference within shared providers. Citations should not be added until verified against the exact claims.

No direct clinical-quality measure, platform or clinician resource cost, patient monetary travel cost, randomized saturation contrast, or mature-platform evidence is available. These gaps prevent claims about clinical quality, net social welfare, causal displacement, systemwide access expansion, or steady-state effects.

## Quality-control status

All numerical values in the manuscript trace to the supplied evidence packet. The abstract is within the requested 180–250-word range, and the introduction is within the requested approximate 1,500–2,000-word range. The draft is manuscript prose but is not citation-ready or submission-ready. Recommended next action: stop unless verified literature or new capacity, quality, cost, or longer-run evidence is supplied.
