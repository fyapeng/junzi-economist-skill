# Round 16 — structural estimation, profiles, and executable failure evidence

## Purpose

Test structural work as an auditable chain from the data-generating process through estimation, numerical acceptance, identification language, counterfactual support, and branch decisions. Three initial blind endpoints covered dynamic discrete choice, differentiated-products demand and supply, and latent-type misspecification. Failures had to remain visible, generate general rules at the correct layer, and survive fresh exact-commit transfer.

## Initial endpoints and rule revisions

`JE-X044` reproduced the dynamic model but called a finite population grid stronger identification evidence than it was, omitted profile starts, and used production functions in its verifier. `JE-X045` recovered demand, markups, costs, and counterfactual equilibria, yet retained a latent-cost-conditioned market-drop branch and incomplete failure accounting. `JE-X046` reproduced its mixture estimators but judged constrained convergence with raw gradients, admitted a failed optimum to Wald coverage, omitted start traces, and described proposed profiles as evidence.

Commit `e25b4d0` added continuous-versus-grid identification, projected/KKT inference eligibility, declared supports, unconditional simulated populations, invalid-instrument timing, scaled equilibrium residuals, all-start records, executable failure preservation, and independent-verifier scope. The first transfers (`JE-X047`–`JE-X049`) improved all three designs but exposed new defects: a soft-penalty alternative was slightly infeasible, instrument timing disagreed with execution, failures returned success status, unresolved profile points were treated as likelihood exclusions, and inferior local starts entered a policy-set image.

Commits `c467e60`, `77aa2f1`, `539a1e8`, and `f120979` therefore required accepted conditional optima at every reported profile point, best-accepted selection before set mapping, executable instrument timing, nonzero process failure, hard restrictions over the actual closed domain, explicit verifier coverage, round-trip diagnostic precision, unrestricted-likelihood LR references, and evaluated-grid language.

## Clean endpoints

`JE-X053` is the clean demand-supply transfer. Its independent audit reproduced the unconditional finite population, valid and invalid instrument timing, every accepted and rejected start, full-precision round-trip diagnostics, equilibrium residuals, accounting levels, and distinct nonzero simulation, equilibrium, and estimation exits.

`JE-X058` is the clean profile transfer. A raw-primitives-only verifier and a separate audit rebuilt the unrestricted optimum and all fifteen conditional problems from fresh starts, replayed all eighty-one production starts, reproduced projected/KKT decisions, distinguished resolved LR exclusions from holes, and matched censoring and the selected-only policy image.

## Preserved dynamic failures

The dynamic transfers deliberately remain visible. `JE-X055` published stale cross-artifact CCP results; `JE-X057` relied too heavily on objective-reduction stopping; `JE-X059` used uninitialized probabilities for an intermediate policy and duplicated the same bug in its verifier. `JE-X060` repaired arbitrary-policy evaluation and `JE-X061` substantively reproduces cells, estimators, gradients, local rank, grid, policies, accounting, hashes, and chronology. Its overall audit remains mixed only because its coverage wording calls finite saved levels arbitrary and does not bind each policy key to its level explicitly.

## Decision

Keep `structural_estimation` partial for one more round. Demand-supply equilibrium and latent profiles have clean passes, and the dynamic numerical core is strong, but the release-candidate standard requires one clean dynamic transfer whose verifier coverage language exactly matches its executed checks. Preserve every mixed and failed endpoint because they document why the final rules exist. Keep `computation_and_instruments` partial: this round materially strengthened optimization, equilibrium, failure signaling, and independent verification, while broader data construction, parallel execution, and hardware variation remain unfinished.
