# Round 14 — contracts, production, games, and welfare benchmarks

## Purpose

Close the microeconomic-law gap with independent formal behavior tests rather than a textbook checklist. Three isolated endpoints tested hidden-action contracts, production and fixed-cost shutdown, and a strategic-complementarity adoption game. Every endpoint had to derive a complete correspondence, preserve equality and nonexistence, compare private equilibrium with a social benchmark, and execute a deterministic falsification program.

## Contract endpoint

`JE-X034` correctly derived the weak least-cost limited-liability contracts, mode ties, social opportunity-cost benchmark, and rent wedge, but its first audit was `mixed`: it claimed strict participation always made the high-effort minimum unattained. Positive limited-liability rent can instead make participation strict at the weak-IC minimum. It also failed to propagate a strict-IC infimum into nonexistence of the principal's maximizer. The response and verifier returned to the feasible-contract layer, preserved the failure, and passed two retests.

Those lessons became general micro-law rules: separate weak and strict IC from weak and strict participation; distinguish minima from infima; and filter a principal's numerical payoff supremum by whether a contract actually attains it. `JE-X037` then tested an independently worded insurer-audit problem on exact commit `9a3e5fe`. Both blind auditors passed it without revision, and the exact-rational verifier covered all four convention cells, `p_0=0`, equality, mode ties, nonexistence, and the social wedge.

## Production endpoint

`JE-X035` derived Cobb–Douglas conditional demands and unit cost, treated shutdown as a separate branch under an avoidable fixed cost, and established every monopoly and planner entry threshold. It identified the strict no-entry/entry wedge, the conditional monopoly output distortion, and a single nonduplicated resource account. Two audits passed. They corrected only a boundary adjective: Cobb–Douglas is weakly increasing on the closed nonnegative domain and strictly increasing in one input only when the other is positive. The derivation and boundary-enriched verifier were unchanged.

## Game endpoint

`JE-X036` gave the complete pure, mixed, equality, and fully degenerate Nash correspondence. It separated risk dominance and basin language from an actual selection law; derived the full welfare correspondence; distinguished coordination failure from subsidy-induced excess adoption; and showed that the subsidy guaranteeing strict uniqueness may have only an infimum. With a real financing cost it retained set-valued equilibrium welfare and an explicit counterexample in which equilibrium-changing subsidy lowers welfare. Two independent audits passed without revision.

## Decision

Mark `microeconomic_law` proved for the release-candidate audit. The three missing families—contracts, production, and games with equilibrium multiplicity—now have formal, executable, dual-audited evidence, and the contract failure generated a clean committed transfer test. Remaining topics are breadth expansions rather than the previously missing foundation.
