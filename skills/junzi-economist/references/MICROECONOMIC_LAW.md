# Microeconomic law — 微观经济学之法

Load the relevant section to discipline a live mechanism. This is a compact derivation map, not a catalogue of named models.

## Choice and revealed restrictions

Define the object of choice, feasible set, information set, horizon, and source of variation before assigning preferences. Distinguish preference change from constraint, composition, information, or measurement change; ex ante choice from ex post outcome; observed choice from welfare; and individual behavior from aggregate demand.

Use optimization only when it yields restrictions that could fail. Check existence, corner solutions, comparative statics, and whether observed variation identifies primitives or only a local response.

## Production, costs, and organizations

Specify outputs and quality as well as inputs. Separate technology from prices, utilization, capacity, managerial choice, and accounting conventions. Ask whether the objective is profit, survival, mission, budget maximization, political support, professional duty, or a combination.

Dual cost or profit representations require maintained regularity conditions. Productivity residuals may also contain quality, markups, utilization, selection, and measurement error.

## Uncertainty, time, and learning

Write the state, action, shock, transition, belief, and continuation value. Determine which shocks are known when choices are made. Separate risk, ambiguity, learning, anticipation, irreversibility, and commitment. Dynamic behavior can arise from state dependence, switching costs, persistent heterogeneity, expectations, or adjustment frictions; discriminate among them.

## Information, contracts, and mechanism design

Map who knows, observes, verifies, commits, transfers, and enforces. Ask which allocation is feasible under participation, incentive, resource, and institutional constraints. Distinguish adverse selection, moral hazard, signaling, screening, multitasking, relational enforcement, incomplete contracting, and organizational authority.

Contract terms are equilibrium objects. Correlation between incentives and outcomes does not reveal a contract effect without addressing selection and endogenous design.

## Strategic interaction and industrial organization

Define players, timing, actions, information, payoffs, market boundaries, and equilibrium concept. Determine whether price, quantity, quality, capacity, entry, search, matching, bargaining, auctions, platforms, or networks carry the strategic margin.

An equilibrium prediction also requires attention to existence, multiplicity, selection, and comparative statics. Conduct parameters, markups, diversion ratios, and demand elasticities answer different questions and require different variation.

## General equilibrium and welfare

Track feasibility, market clearing, ownership, transfers, externalities, public goods, missing markets, market power, information, and enforcement. The welfare theorems are conditional benchmarks, not default descriptions.

For policy, trace direct behavior, price and wage adjustment, entry and investment, fiscal incidence, congestion, spillovers, distribution, and transition. State the social objective and whose utility, rights, capabilities, or losses it omits.

## Microeconomic stopping test

Before moving to an estimator, state the agents and feasible choices; binding constraints; information and timing; interaction and outcome concept; comparative-static or equilibrium prediction; a rival mechanism; discriminating evidence; and the welfare object if claimed. If these cannot be stated, the bottleneck remains in Fa rather than Shu.
