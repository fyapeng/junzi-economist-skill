# Macroeconomic law — 宏观经济学之法

Use this map to connect individual and institutional behavior to aggregate dynamics. Do not infer a representative economy from averages.

## Begin with accounting and closure

Write the relevant stocks, flows, balance sheets, resource constraints, and sectoral identities. Accounting identities constrain possible stories but do not identify causal mechanisms.

State how households, firms, government, financial intermediaries, the monetary authority, and the external sector are closed. Track nominal versus real quantities, gross versus net flows, levels versus growth, and ex ante plans versus ex post identities.

## Aggregation and heterogeneity

Ask which micro objects aggregate, under what distribution, prices, market completeness, and mobility. Aggregate change may reflect within-unit behavior, composition, entry and exit, reallocation, price indices, or measurement. Representative-agent shortcuts require an aggregation argument and may erase incidence, precaution, or financial amplification.

## Intertemporal constraints and dynamics

Identify state variables, laws of motion, expectations, terminal or transversality conditions, and intertemporal budget constraints. Separate transition dynamics from balanced growth or steady state. A long-run relation need not describe adjustment, and a short-run multiplier need not persist.

## Growth, development, and structural transformation

Distinguish accumulation, innovation, diffusion, human capability, institutions, demographics, misallocation, sectoral reallocation, and global integration. Ask whether growth change is a level effect, transitional acceleration, permanent trend change, or measurement revision.

Development is not GDP alone. Examine productive capacity, health, education, structural dependence, distribution, ecological constraints, and effective human agency.

## Fluctuations and propagation

Separate impulses from propagation. Shocks may be technological, demand, policy, financial, uncertainty, commodity, external, health, or institutional. Propagation may operate through nominal rigidity, balance sheets, inventories, search, networks, expectations, capacity, or policy feedback.

Labeling a residual a shock does not establish an autonomous disturbance. Check anticipation, news, endogenous policy, simultaneous adjustment, and measurement.

## Money, finance, and fiscal systems

Trace consolidated and unconsolidated balance sheets when relevant. State the nominal anchor, payment and liquidity mechanism, credit creation, collateral, default, maturity, and policy rule. For fiscal policy, account for financing, timing, incidence, implementation capacity, expectations, and monetary interaction.

Neither money neutrality nor persistent real effects should be assumed outside their horizon, friction, and policy regime.

## Labor, open, and spatial adjustment

Connect employment, hours, participation, vacancies, matching, bargaining, migration, informality, and household production. In open and spatial settings, add trade balances, exchange rates, capital flows, trade costs, location, housing, transport, local public goods, and mobility constraints.

## Macro policy stopping test

Before recommending a model or estimator, state the aggregate target and accounting definition; micro and institutional mechanisms; aggregation and distribution assumptions; stocks, flows, expectations, and policy regime; equilibrium closure; transition and long-run horizons; rival propagation mechanisms; and incidence, welfare, and uncertainty.

If a conclusion changes with composition, regime, or balance-sheet closure, report that dependence rather than presenting one aggregate coefficient as structural.
