# Economic writing — 从研究状态到论文表达

Writing transmits an economic judgment; it cannot improve the evidence status of that judgment. Load this module only when a research output must be drafted, revised, translated, positioned, or reviewed.

## Gate writing by research state

Before drafting, identify:

- the economic object and live question;
- claim versions and statuses in the claim ledger;
- authoritative tables, figures, equations, model output, and institutional sources;
- the intended artifact: exploratory note, scaffold, working paper, manuscript section, referee report, presentation, or public explanation;
- audience, language, outlet constraints, and requested length.

If the question, mechanism, identification, derivation, or result version is unresolved, write a labeled scaffold or diagnostic memo. Do not make the prose sound manuscript-ready.

## Build the paper spine

A paper normally needs this causal and argumentative spine, adapted to its type:

1. economic question or tension;
2. why existing theory or evidence leaves it unresolved;
3. setting, model, data, or variation that creates leverage;
4. method and its identifying or derivational logic;
5. main result with magnitude, theorem, or bounded characterization;
6. mechanism and rival explanations;
7. counterfactual, welfare, or broader implication within its support;
8. contribution relative to the closest verified literature.

Description, theory, reduced-form, structural, historical, and mixed-method papers need different emphases. Do not force every paper into an empirical-treatment template.

## Write sections by their economic job

### Abstract

State the object, leverage, main finding, and bounded update. Include a mechanism, counterfactual, or welfare result only when supported. Do not lead with a contribution claim or broad importance slogan.

### Introduction

Move from the economic problem to the unresolved mechanism or policy target, structural environment, evidence that disciplines it, central result, interpretation, and literature position. Present the main result before a long review. For a structural paper, explain early how descriptive and reduced-form evidence motivates, identifies, benchmarks, or validates the model, and which equilibrium, welfare, or counterfactual conclusion the integrated structure makes possible.

### Setting and data

Include institutional and measurement facts needed to understand the mechanism, identifying variation, population, market, and data-generating process. State old and new rules precisely. Separate written policy, implementation, exposure, and measured treatment.

### Empirical strategy

Explain the source of variation, counterfactual, estimand, population, assumptions, and failure conditions before notation. Fixed effects, controls, or estimator names do not establish identification.

### Theory or structural model

Present economic environment, timing, information, primitives, feasible choices, equilibrium or solution concept, derivation or identification, validation, and the policy object. Mark every formal result as verified, conditional, local, conjectural, or failed before polishing it.

### Results

Lead with the economic fact, then magnitude and benchmark, interpretation, rival explanation, limitation, and next discriminating evidence. Do not narrate tables column by column. A null result may constrain a mechanism without supporting equivalence or absence.

### Conclusion

State what belief changed, the strongest supported implication, and the material uncertainty that remains. Do not turn limited evidence into generic policy advocacy.

## Maintain claim, literature, and style separation

Use three distinct ledgers when needed:

- **Claim ledger:** what the project can currently say and why.
- **Literature ledger:** verified works, exact citation job, supported claim, and verification status.
- **Style ledger:** paragraph jobs, pacing, sentence patterns, and citation choreography learned from exemplars.

A style exemplar supplies no substantive citation permission. A nearby paper supplies no claim merely because it shares a keyword, country, dataset, policy label, or method.

Organize literature by intellectual role: foundational theory, closest mechanism, empirical measurement, identification, institutional source, model precedent, counterfactual method, contrasting result, or context. Write claim-first synthesis; use author-as-subject when the paper or method is genuinely the object.

Verify title, authors, year, outlet, DOI or stable identifier, and the exact claim before citation. Mark uncertain entries `VERIFY`. Retrieve exact text before quoting.

## Reconstruct formal claims before writing

For theory and structural work, check:

1. players, timing, information, state and choice domains;
2. objectives, constraints, policy rules, and equilibrium concept;
3. existence, uniqueness, boundaries, FOCs or KKT conditions, and global versus local results;
4. which assumptions determine each sign or welfare ranking;
5. identification of each estimated primitive;
6. targeted and untargeted fit, external validation, and counterfactual support.

Use symbolic and numerical tools to search for counterexamples. A computation can challenge a proposition; it cannot by itself prove a general result.

## Use restrained human prose

- Name agents, choices, prices, constraints, mechanisms, estimates, and units.
- Prefer direct claims and active economic verbs.
- Match causal and welfare qualifiers to the design.
- Avoid inflated importance claims, vague “mechanisms,” policy-report slogans, and repeated contrast formulas such as “不是……而是……”.
- Fix logic before polishing sentences.
- Preserve differences between Chinese and English academic syntax without changing the economic claim.

Do not imitate an author's distinctive phrasing. Extract paragraph function and reasoning rhythm, then write in the project's own language.

## Review and stop

Review in this order:

1. object and claim-version consistency;
2. evidence, derivation, and citation integrity;
3. section function and argumentative sequence;
4. magnitude, notation, table, and figure consistency;
5. language, length, and outlet formatting.

Stop revising when only cosmetic preferences remain and no named failure, new evidence, or requested section justifies another loop. Return a revision judgment: stop, minor revision, major revision, or backtrack to research.
