# Bilingual economic writing — 双语经济学表达

Use this module when an economic argument, paper section, research note, abstract, referee response, or public explanation must move between Chinese and English. Translation preserves the research state while rebuilding the prose for the target language and audience.

## Keep one economic object

Before drafting, designate the authoritative claim and result sources. Record the population, institution, treatment or policy margin, estimand, comparison, mechanism status, welfare boundary, result version, units, and claim status. Neither language version becomes more authoritative merely because it is more polished.

Use `assets/templates/BILINGUAL_CLAIM_MAP.yaml` when the output contains multiple material claims, many numbers, or parallel manuscript sections. Maintain a compact term and notation ledger for concepts whose translation could alter the economics.

## Translate claims and paragraph jobs

Translate at the level of an economic claim or paragraph job, not word by word:

1. identify what the source passage is doing—defining the object, stating variation, reporting a magnitude, interpreting a mechanism, locating literature, or bounding a conclusion;
2. preserve the evidence anchor and claim status;
3. rebuild sentence order, subjects, transitions, and paragraph rhythm for the target language;
4. compare the completed target passage against the source claim map.

Do not carry source-language vagueness into the target text. Resolve an ambiguous antecedent, policy label, comparison group, or unit from the authoritative research artifacts; if it cannot be resolved, mark the ambiguity instead of guessing.

## Chinese to English

Build English paragraphs around agents, choices, constraints, variation, magnitudes, and belief updates. Convert broad Chinese labels into the exact economic object. Replace generic claims of significance with the supported allocation, incentive, incidence, fiscal, equilibrium, or welfare implication.

Distinguish carefully among:

- `mechanism`, `channel`, `economic force`, and `mediator`;
- treatment-effect heterogeneity, differential response, and subgroup association;
- policy implication, design implication, welfare implication, and external-validity limit;
- reform, rule change, price-function change, implementation, exposure, and treatment receipt.

Do not use English prestige style to strengthen causality, novelty, mechanism, robustness, or welfare beyond the Chinese evidence.

## English to Chinese

Preserve causal qualifiers, assumptions, timing, magnitudes, units, uncertainty, reference groups, and claim boundaries. Use compact academic Chinese organized by economic logic. Avoid adding administrative-report language, policy slogans, or ornate importance claims absent from the source.

Translate technical terms consistently but follow context. For example, `identification` may concern identification strategy, parameter identification, or set identification; `welfare` may denote a stated model criterion rather than general social benefit. Record consequential choices in the term ledger.

## Parallel outputs

For dual-language delivery:

1. establish one claim–evidence map and one authoritative result version;
2. choose each output's audience, artifact type, and paragraph jobs;
3. draft the language best suited to the immediate research purpose;
4. write the parallel version from the common map rather than translating the finished sentences mechanically;
5. reconcile both versions claim by claim.

The two versions may differ in syntax, length, signposting, and background explanation. They must agree on the economic object, sample, estimand, assumptions, signs, magnitudes, units, uncertainty, mechanism status, citations, and policy or welfare boundary.

## Translate papers without merging voices

When translating a paper abstract or deep-reading note, preserve the separation among author claim, demonstrated result, reader inference, and open conjecture. A fluent translation does not authorize a stronger author claim or convert the reader's extension into the paper's conclusion. Summarize copyrighted text in original language unless a short exact translation is necessary and permitted.

## Reconciliation gate

Before handoff, check:

- every material claim has the same status and evidence anchor in both languages;
- coefficients, signs, units, samples, dates, equation notation, table and figure numbers, and citekeys agree;
- causal, external-validity, mechanism, counterfactual, and welfare qualifiers survive;
- translated terminology does not silently change the agent, margin, timing, comparison, or solution concept;
- each artifact is labeled as manuscript prose, translation, scaffold, note, review, or public explanation.

When versions conflict, return to the common evidence map. Use the strongest wording supported in each language only when the meanings remain equivalent; otherwise preserve the narrower common claim and record the unresolved translation decision. Stop when the economic content reconciles and only stylistic preference remains.
