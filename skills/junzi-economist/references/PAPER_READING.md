# Paper reading — 从论文文本到可复核的研究理解

Read a paper as a structured set of economic claims, evidence, assumptions, and unresolved questions. Do not treat its abstract, journal placement, equations, or polished prose as proof.

## Select the reading purpose

Clarify the output before extracting everything:

- **triage:** decide relevance and next reading priority;
- **study:** reconstruct theory, identification, results, and limitations;
- **literature mapping:** compare mechanisms, objects, evidence, and boundaries;
- **replication:** recover data, transformations, estimands, equations, code, and computational choices;
- **seminar or referee:** identify contribution, strongest evidence, decisive weakness, and questions;
- **public explanation:** preserve accuracy while reducing notation and field-specific assumptions.

Do not impose one fixed article structure. Omit sections that the paper does not have.

## Establish the source

Record title, authors, version date, outlet or working-paper status, DOI or stable URL, and the actual file read. Distinguish preprint, accepted manuscript, published version, appendix, replication package, and later correction. Verify current publication metadata online when it may have changed.

Respect copyright: summarize in original language, quote briefly, and use exact text only when needed and permitted.

## Reconstruct the paper

Read beyond the abstract. Recover the relevant subset of:

1. real phenomenon and economic question;
2. agents, constraints, information, interaction, institution, and welfare object;
3. contribution claimed relative to which literature;
4. data, population, market, timing, measurement, and sample construction;
5. estimand, variation, comparison, assumptions, and threats;
6. model primitives, equilibrium, identification, computation, and counterfactuals;
7. main results with units, uncertainty, and benchmark;
8. mechanism evidence and serious rivals;
9. robustness, validation, nulls, contradictions, and omitted tests;
10. what the authors conclude and what the evidence independently permits.

Preserve exact equations only when they carry the identification, model, or result. Define every term and explain the economic job of the equation. Do not copy notation that is irrelevant to the reading purpose.

For empirical equations, transcribe the interaction order, variable transformation, reference group, and inference procedure exactly. Do not call a two-way interaction a triple difference merely because its coefficient compares two differences. Distinguish continuous variables from indicators such as above-median transforms, and heteroskedasticity-robust standard errors from clustering. Recheck these labels against the equation, table notes, and appendix before finalizing the note.

## Separate four voices

Label distinctions that matter:

- **Author claim:** what the paper says.
- **Demonstrated result:** what follows from the reported derivation or evidence under stated assumptions.
- **Reader inference:** a warranted interpretation not stated directly.
- **Open conjecture:** an extension, mechanism, policy implication, or research idea still needing evidence.

Do not convert author language into demonstrated fact. Do not conceal your own extrapolation inside the summary.

## Audit by paper type

### Descriptive or measurement

Check object definition, coverage, missingness, construction choices, comparability, validation, and which theories the fact disciplines. Do not demand a treatment effect when measurement is the contribution.

### Causal empirical

Check assignment or variation, counterfactual, estimand, treatment timing, anticipation, spillovers, composition, inference, and external validity. Reconstruct the main equation and coefficient interpretation.

### Theory

Check primitives, timing, information, solution concept, assumptions, existence, uniqueness, boundary cases, proposition status, proof logic, comparative statics, and empirical content.

### Structural or quantitative

Separate economic environment, identification, functional form, estimation, computation, targeted fit, untargeted validation, policy support, and welfare. Convergence and fit do not establish identification or counterfactual validity.

### Historical, institutional, or qualitative

Check sequence, categories in their historical context, source selection, process evidence, alternatives, implementation, power, and how evidence discriminates among mechanisms. Do not force all evidence into a regression hierarchy.

## Build a literature map

For each relevant line, record the shared object, mechanism, evidence, contribution, boundary, and unresolved tension. Distinguish foundational theory, closest comparison, method precedent, institutional context, replication, contradiction, and adjacent discipline.

A paper's reference list is a search lead, not a verified literature map. Trace important claims to original sources and search for later corrections or rival evidence.

## Produce durable notes

A technical note normally contains:

- bibliographic identity and version;
- one-paragraph research judgment;
- question and economic structure;
- theory or identification reconstruction;
- main results and claim status;
- limitations, rivals, and failure conditions;
- relation to the reader's project;
- follow-up evidence or reading.

Use tables or equations only when they clarify comparisons. For public explanation, reduce technical detail but retain the counterfactual, uncertainty, and boundary of the result.

If a figure or table matters and cannot be reproduced lawfully, refer to its number and explain its role rather than fabricating a placeholder image.
