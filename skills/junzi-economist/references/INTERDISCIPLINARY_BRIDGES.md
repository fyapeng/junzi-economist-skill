# Interdisciplinary bridges — 跨学科求真

Use this module when an economic question depends on facts, categories, mechanisms, or values governed partly by another field. Preserve economics as the organizing language for choice, allocation, interaction, equilibrium, distribution, and welfare while allowing outside knowledge to correct what economics has represented badly or omitted.

## Name the bridge before searching

State the economic object and the exact uncertainty that another discipline may resolve. Classify the intended bridge:

- **ontology:** change what the relevant actor, state, event, outcome, or institution is;
- **constraint:** change the feasible set, technology, law, biology, geography, capacity, or sequence;
- **mechanism:** add or defeat a behavioral, organizational, political, ecological, or technical channel;
- **measurement:** change how a construct can be observed, classified, linked, timed, or interpreted;
- **identification:** reveal selection, interference, implementation, anticipation, or a direct channel;
- **evaluation:** add harms, rights, meanings, risks, distribution, or affected groups to the welfare account;
- **rival:** supply an explanation that can match the same economic fact.

Do not begin with a list of disciplines. Begin with a live defect or rival in the economic account.

## Respect epistemic jurisdiction

Use each field for claims it can warrant. Do not ask one discipline to certify another discipline's conclusion.

- **Medicine and public health:** establish biological processes, clinical states, care pathways, risks, and outcome meaning. Clinical plausibility does not identify an economic policy effect.
- **Law and political science:** establish authority, jurisdiction, rights, rulemaking, enforcement, discretion, contestation, and implementation. A statute's text does not prove compliance or behavioral incidence.
- **History:** establish sequence, contingency, institutional origins, changing categories, and path dependence through source criticism. A historical analogy does not by itself transport a causal magnitude.
- **Sociology and anthropology:** establish organizations, networks, norms, stratification, roles, meanings, and lived constraints. Do not compress these automatically into fixed preferences.
- **Psychology and behavioral science:** establish attention, cognition, learning, perception, and experimentally bounded behavior. Check task, population, setting, duration, and equilibrium feedback before importing a parameter.
- **Geography and transportation:** establish spatial support, accessibility, travel, networks, boundaries, exposure, and mobility. Administrative proximity is not necessarily effective access or a market.
- **Computer science and operations research:** establish algorithms, information systems, queues, optimization, and technical feasibility. Treat an algorithm's objective, training data, constraints, and governance as parts of an allocation institution.
- **Environmental and natural sciences:** establish physical stocks and flows, exposure, thresholds, irreversibility, uncertainty, and ecological constraints. Monetary valuation does not erase non-substitutability or scientific uncertainty.

Prefer primary field evidence, authoritative syntheses, institutional records, and qualified expert judgment according to the claim. Preserve version, jurisdiction, population, setting, and disagreement. Escalate to a domain expert when a safety-critical, rights-bearing, clinical, legal, or technically specialized conclusion exceeds the available evidence.

## Translate without flattening

For every consequential imported proposition, record:

1. the outside-field statement in its own terms;
2. its source, scope, uncertainty, and serious disagreement;
3. the economic element it changes—agent, state, objective, constraint, information, technology, institution, interaction, dynamics, observable, or welfare object;
4. at least one alternative economic translation;
5. the new observable implication or design consequence;
6. what remains outside the economic model.

Use `assets/templates/INTERDISCIPLINARY_BRIDGE.yaml` when several imported propositions govern a project. Keep the original concept alongside the economic representation. Terms such as quality, risk, discrimination, consent, resilience, access, power, severity, and welfare often carry field-specific content that one scalar cannot preserve.

## Rebuild the economic account

Start from the baseline economic mechanism, then revise only the links affected by outside knowledge. Make the change inspectable:

```text
outside-field proposition
    → revised economic object or link
    → changed behavior / interaction / dynamics
    → discriminating observation
    → claim and welfare consequence
```

Derive how the revised model differs from both the baseline and its strongest rival. Outside knowledge can invalidate a premise even when it does not supply a complete replacement model. When disciplines use incompatible units or values, preserve the conflict and report conditional conclusions rather than forcing a synthetic index.

## Keep evidence roles separate

Distinguish four evidentiary jobs:

- evidence that an economic assumption is false or incomplete;
- evidence that a response channel is feasible;
- evidence that actors used the channel in the setting studied;
- evidence that the channel caused the observed outcome.

The first two can require major model revision without establishing the latter two. Carry source uncertainty into claim status. Do not turn an outside-field correlation, mechanism, classification, narrative, or expert consensus into an identified economic effect.

## Let the bridge change research practice

A genuine bridge should alter at least one of the following: question, state space, feasible set, mechanism, market or institutional boundary, variable definition, data collection, estimand, research design, counterfactual, welfare account, or claim boundary. If it changes only the literature-review paragraph, remove it.

Seek affected people's knowledge when administrative and scholarly categories may omit relevant choices, constraints, meanings, or harms. Treat testimony and participation according to their evidentiary role; do not claim representativeness or causal identification they cannot supply.

Backtrack to **Shi** when jurisdiction, implementation, sequence, or field facts were misunderstood. Backtrack to **Fa** when they invalidate the agents, constraints, mechanism, equilibrium, or welfare object. Redesign **Shu** when they change measurement or identification. Stop searching once the remaining interdisciplinary uncertainty cannot change the economic model, claim status, research design, safety boundary, or next discriminating action.
