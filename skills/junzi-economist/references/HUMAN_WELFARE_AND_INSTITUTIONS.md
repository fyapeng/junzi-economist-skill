# Human welfare and institutions

## Begin from real people

Identify who chooses, who is represented by the model, who is affected, and who is absent from the data. Do not reduce patients, workers, students, households, or communities to treatment labels or demand units when agency, vulnerability, or institutional dependence matters.

Reconstruct the feasible set before interpreting observed participation as preference, consent, or welfare. Separate an actor's background dependence and limited outside options from penalties, opacity, retaliation, or control imposed by another actor. Dependence alone does not make every constrained choice coercive; their interaction can still weaken voluntariness and bargaining. Treat hypothetical survey answers as stated preference under the described scenario, not direct revelation of true preference, and preserve hypothetical, sampling, wording, information, and fear-of-disclosure limits.

## Separate positive and normative claims

- Positive analysis explains behavior, constraints, interactions, and outcomes.
- Normative analysis requires an explicit welfare criterion, interpersonal and intertemporal treatment, and distributional accounting.
- A market equilibrium, willingness to pay, cost saving, or average treatment effect is not by itself a social welfare judgment.
- Evidence can discipline consequences without choosing distributional weights or deciding whether a right is tradable. A proposed pilot, safeguard, mandate, or prohibition remains a **conditional normative or research recommendation** under stated criteria; never give it an empirical claim status such as `supported` merely because its design is feasible. Keep reported accounting changes provisional until definitions, baselines, shifted costs, composition, and uncertainty are audited.

## Welfare checklist

For consequential policy analysis, examine:

- direct benefits and harms;
- prices, taxes, transfers, and fiscal incidence;
- quality, access, waiting, and nonprice rationing;
- risk, insurance, and option value;
- producer, worker, intermediary, and government outcomes;
- heterogeneity and distribution;
- entry, exit, investment, innovation, and long-run adaptation;
- dignity, autonomy, rights, and unpriced effects;
- general-equilibrium and cross-market spillovers;
- uncertainty and model dependence.

Maintain one social account. Separate real resource gains and costs from prices, wages, transfers, accounting profit, producer surplus, and economic rents; assign ownership and fiscal incidence; include external harms; and do not count the same payment once in individual surplus and again as a resource gain. For Pareto claims, require joint individual causal welfare changes sufficient to show nobody is worse off. Missing no-loser evidence makes Pareto improvement **not established or unsupported**; it does not contradict the claim unless evidence establishes at least one loser. Keep actual Pareto improvement distinct from Kaldor–Hicks potential compensation and from compensation that was actually paid.

Do not call profit itself a transfer or a rent. A payment has a payer and recipient and may cancel in an unweighted resource account; profit is producer surplus accruing to owners after the accounting costs recognized by the firm. Decompose accounting profit into opportunity costs and normal returns, economic rent or market-power incidence, taxes, real productivity changes, and costs shifted to workers, users, households, or the public before drawing welfare conclusions.

When experimentation affects agency, safety, privacy, or due process, specify a common nonrandomizable protection floor for every arm. Vary interventions only above that floor, with meaningful information, refusal, exit, nonretaliation, and access-neutral consent where relevant. Do not make the withdrawal of basic protection a treatment arm.

## Institutions are endogenous constraints

Rules operate through enforcement, administrative capacity, organizational incentives, political authority, and informal norms. Investigate who designs, controls, interprets, and can evade the institution. Model formal rules and implementation separately when needed.

## Human concern without moral display

Human concern changes research objects and evaluation; it is not a concluding paragraph. It may require different outcomes, subgroup analysis, institutional investigation, model constraints, or policy objectives. Do not claim to represent affected groups without evidence or participation.

## Cross-disciplinary correction

Use medical, legal, sociological, psychological, historical, and political knowledge to correct economic misspecification. Preserve the economic question while allowing other disciplines to reveal omitted constraints, mechanisms, meanings, and harms.
