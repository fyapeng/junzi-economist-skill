# Source protocol — 来源核验

Use this compact protocol for a specific source, quotation, citation, or provenance question. Load the broader `SOURCE_MAP.md` only when intellectual lineage or study coverage is itself the task.

## Identify the source job

Determine whether the source is needed for a definition, theorem, mechanism, empirical fact, institutional rule, method, dataset, or software behavior. One source may perform several jobs, but each manuscript sentence needs a specific warrant.

## Verify before using

1. Prefer the original work for the claim and a modern synthesis for scope and later qualifications.
2. Retrieve exact text before quoting; never reconstruct a quotation from memory.
3. Record author, title, version or edition, year, chapter or section, page when stable, and DOI or durable URL when available.
4. Distinguish preprint, accepted manuscript, published version, appendix, correction, replication package, and later revision.
5. Check whether the cited passage supports the exact sentence, population, mechanism, and boundary.
6. Search for serious rivals, failures, replications, corrections, and subsequent revisions when the claim is consequential.
7. Separate “this source argues” from “the evidence supports.”

## Place sources by layer

- Durable theory, accounting, and modes of inquiry mainly shape Fa.
- Field models and research procedures refine Fa and Shu.
- Current papers, replications, institutional documents, data manuals, and software documentation establish Shi and Qi and require current retrieval.

No bibliography proves the frontier. Search outward from the live economic mechanism and stop when another source is unlikely to change the claim, design, or next discriminating test.

## Cross-disciplinary correction

Use primary field sources when biological, legal, organizational, spatial, psychological, historical, or technological facts constrain the economic model. State which agent, constraint, mechanism, measurement, equilibrium, or welfare conclusion changes because of the outside discipline.

Separate three uses of outside evidence: a fact that defeats an economic assumption, a feasible response channel that must enter the model, and evidence that actors actually used that channel under the policy. Do not promote a plausible selection, coding, clinical, legal, or organizational response into an established causal effect merely because the revised model permits it.

Do not cite an adjacent discipline as decoration or treat disciplinary prestige as evidence.
