# Theory router

Use this router after naming the real phenomenon. Load only the theoretical families that can materially alter the research question or evidence.

| Phenomenon | Begin with | Ask next | Common rivals |
|---|---|---|---|
| Household or individual choice | constrained choice, risk, time, information | feasible set, beliefs, switching and search costs | preferences, constraints, information, measurement |
| Firm price, quality, or product choice | production/cost, demand, competition | market boundary, strategic timing, capacity | cost, demand, conduct, regulation |
| Entry, exit, or investment | sunk costs, option value, dynamic games | expectations, state transition, commitment | selection, financing, policy, demand trends |
| Healthcare use and provider behavior | demand, physician agency, insurance, capacity, quality | patient information, referral, payment, congestion | preferences, access, severity, provider incentives |
| Education choice and outcomes | human capital, signaling, peer effects, school production | dynamic investment, information, matching | selection, resources, incentives, sorting |
| Labor-market transitions | search/matching, contracts, human capital, discrimination | vacancies, bargaining, mobility, networks | productivity, preferences, institutions, demand |
| Contracts, reporting, compliance | principal-agent, screening, multitasking, enforcement | observability, verifiability, renegotiation | norms, selection, capacity, political control |
| Platforms and networks | two-sided markets, network effects, matching, information | governance, pricing sides, interoperability | scale, quality, lock-in, selection |
| Public policy and taxation | public finance, incidence, externalities, political economy | behavioral margins, enforcement, equilibrium | targeting, salience, administration, general equilibrium |
| Regional or spatial outcomes | spatial equilibrium, trade, migration, local public goods | mobility, housing, transport, fiscal links | sorting, place effects, agglomeration, policy |
| Aggregate growth or fluctuations | growth, heterogeneous agents, frictions, expectations | aggregation, propagation, policy rules | composition, measurement, structural change |
| Inequality or distribution | incidence, heterogeneity, institutions, bargaining | wealth dynamics, access, power, intergenerational transmission | technology, policy, sorting, discrimination |

## Mechanism construction

For each candidate mechanism, write:

```text
agents → objectives → constraints/information → choices → interaction
       → equilibrium/dynamic outcome → observable implication → welfare
```

Then identify a rival that changes a different link while matching the headline fact.

## Reduced-form and structural bridge

Economic theory should determine the objects to estimate, not predetermine a structural estimator. A theoretical mechanism may be tested with descriptive facts, quasi-experimental variation, experiments, calibrated models, structural estimation, or combinations. Use structural estimation only when latent primitives and equilibrium counterfactuals are essential to the target question.

## Cross-disciplinary bridge

Invite other disciplines when they change agents, constraints, mechanisms, measurement, or evaluation:

- medicine and public health for biological processes and care pathways;
- sociology for norms, networks, stratification, and organizations;
- political science and law for authority, enforcement, and institutional change;
- psychology for cognition, attention, learning, and preferences;
- geography and transportation for space, access, and mobility;
- computer science and operations research for algorithms, queues, optimization, and platform systems;
- history for path dependence, institutional origins, and changing meanings.

Do not add an adjacent discipline as decorative literature. State what economic conclusion changes because of it.
