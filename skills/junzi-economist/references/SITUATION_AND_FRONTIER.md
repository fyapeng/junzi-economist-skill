# Situation and frontier — 察势

## Investigate before formalizing

Establish the concrete environment in which economic mechanisms operate:

- historical stage and policy sequence;
- written rules versus implementation;
- actors, authority, incentives, resources, and outside options;
- market and organizational boundaries;
- prices and nonprice allocation;
- capacity, congestion, networks, and geography;
- who records data, for what administrative purpose, and through which process;
- adaptation, evasion, anticipation, and spillovers;
- affected groups, missing voices, and unmeasured consequences.

Treat institutional detail as part of the economic model, not background decoration.

## Audit the data-generating process

For each core variable, record:

1. Who generated it and why?
2. What event, person, transaction, or stock does it represent?
3. Which decisions determine whether it appears?
4. Did definitions, coding, coverage, incentives, or technology change?
5. What cannot be linked, observed, or distinguished?
6. Could policy alter measurement independently of the economic object?

Do not infer individual paths from aggregates, referrals from geographic composition, quality from utilization, demand from observed quantity, or welfare from spending without an explicit bridge.

When allocation is sequential, observe or explicitly mark each decision margin: recommendation or shortlist, information shown to each actor, patient acceptance or override, provider acceptance or rejection, final allocation, and post-arrival treatment. A final destination cannot by itself separate patient preference, intermediary steering, and receiving-provider rationing.

Keep a proposed source of variation conditional until its assignment rule, timing, manipulation risk, exposure, and direct channels are verified. A policy page that describes an intervention does not establish staggered rollout, a fixed threshold, random exposure, or a valid instrument.

## Map the literature frontier

Search in layers:

1. foundational theory and canonical empirical facts;
2. mature identification and measurement debates;
3. leading field and general-interest publications;
4. recent working papers and conference programs;
5. adjacent disciplines and institutional sources;
6. contradictory, null, and replication evidence.

For every literature lane, extract the question, mechanism, object, evidence, boundary, and unresolved tension. A bibliography is not a frontier map.

## Test novelty

Novelty can arise from a new fact, mechanism, measurement, design, institutional setting with theoretical leverage, equilibrium connection, policy counterfactual, or synthesis that changes understanding. New data, a new country, or a newer method is insufficient by itself.

Ask:

- What prior belief or model would change?
- Which literature currently cannot explain the fact?
- What evidence discriminates the proposed mechanism?
- Is the contribution robust to different terminology and citation sets?

## Diagnose the current research stage

Classify the main bottleneck as one or more of:

- object/question;
- theory/mechanism;
- institution/market boundary;
- measurement/data access;
- identification;
- structural identification;
- computation;
- validation;
- novelty/positioning;
- claim calibration/writing.

Work on the highest upstream unresolved bottleneck. Do not polish prose when the mechanism is undefined or optimize a solver when the parameter is unidentified.
